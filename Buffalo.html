🐃 Buffalo Rescue Guide

🚑 Injured Buffalo Rescue ▶

⚠ Buffaloes are powerful and unpredictable—never approach without professionals!

First aid for injured buffaloes:

  1. Assess from a safe distance—look for limping, bleeding, or labored breathing
  2. Call wildlife/animal control immediately—do NOT attempt to handle alone
  3. For bleeding: use clean towels (if safe) but avoid direct contact
  4. Keep other animals/people away—buffaloes may charge when stressed
  5. Provide water if possible but do not approach

🔥 Heat Stress or Dehydration ▶

Signs of heat stress in buffaloes:

  1. Excessive panting, drooling, or lethargy
  2. Seek shade and provide water in a wide, shallow container
  3. Use sprinklers to cool the ground (buffaloes wallow to regulate temperature)
  4. Avoid cold water shock—use slightly cool water instead
  5. Monitor for signs of heatstroke (collapse, seizures)
🚗 Buffalo on Road or Trapped ▶

Helping a buffalo in traffic or stuck:

  1. Alert authorities immediately—buffaloes can cause severe accidents
  2. Create a barrier with vehicles (park diagonally with hazards on)
  3. Do NOT honk or make loud noises—buffaloes may panic and charge
  4. If trapped in mud/water: call professionals with proper equipment
  5. Never attempt to pull out a stuck buffalo—risk of injury
🐄 Domestic Buffalo Care ▶

Rescuing abandoned or mistreated domestic buffaloes:

  1. Check for tags or brands—contact local farmers if identified
  2. Provide fresh water and high-fiber feed (hay, grass)
  3. Buffaloes need wallowing areas—ensure access to mud/water
  4. Separate injured/sick buffaloes from the herd
  5. Contact livestock rescue organizations for long-term care
⚠ Aggressive Buffalo Handling ▶

Dealing with an aggressive buffalo:

  1. Never turn your back or run—back away slowly
  2. Use barriers (trees, vehicles) to protect yourself
  3. If charged, stand behind a solid object or climb to safety
  4. Buffaloes may bluff charge—stay calm and avoid sudden movements
  5. Call wildlife experts immediately—do NOT attempt to handle
