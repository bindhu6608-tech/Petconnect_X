🐱 Cat Rescue Guide

🚑 Injured Cat Rescue ▶

⚠ Cats hide pain well—assume any injured cat may bite or scratch!

First aid for injured cats:

  1. Approach slowly while speaking softly—avoid direct eye contact
  2. Use a thick towel or blanket to gently wrap the cat (like a burrito)
  3. Check for bleeding, labored breathing, or limping
  4. For bleeding: apply pressure with clean gauze (avoid cotton)
  5. Never give human medication—cats metabolize drugs differently
  6. Transport in a covered carrier to reduce stress

🔥 Cat in Heat or Fire Emergency ▶

If a cat is overheating or in a fire:

  1. Look for panting, drooling, or bright red gums (heatstroke signs)
  2. Cool paws and ears with room-temperature water (never ice)
  3. Offer small amounts of water—cats won't drink when stressed
  4. In fires: check hiding spots (under beds, closets) as cats hide
  5. Use a pillowcase as an emergency carrier if needed
🌳 Tree or High Place Rescue ▶

Helping a cat stuck in a tree or high place:

  1. Most cats can climb down—give them 24 hours to try
  2. Place strong-smelling food (tuna, sardines) at the base
  3. Call professionals—cat claws make ladder rescues dangerous
  4. If the cat is crying non-stop or injured, act faster
  5. Never use water hoses or loud noises—this may cause panic falls
🚗 Stray or Hit-by-Car Cat ▶

Helping a cat on the road:

  1. Park safely—cats often bolt when approached
  2. Use a carrier or box—never hold a scared cat in your arms
  3. Check for "tipped ears" (sign of a neutered feral cat)
  4. For injured cats: slide cardboard under them before moving
  5. Even friendly cats may need 2-3 days to calm down
