🐶 Dog Rescue Guide

🚑 Injured Dog Rescue ▶

⚠️ Always approach an injured dog carefully—they may bite out of fear or pain.

First aid steps for injured dogs:

  1. Stay calm and speak softly to avoid startling the dog.
  2. Check for bleeding, broken bones, or signs of pain.
  3. If the dog is aggressive, use a blanket or towel to gently restrain them.
  4. Apply pressure to stop bleeding with a clean cloth.
  5. Do NOT attempt to set broken bones—stabilize and transport carefully.
  6. Call a vet immediately.

🔥 Dog in Heat or Fire Emergency ▶

If a dog is overheating or in a fire:

  1. Move the dog to a shaded or cool area.
  2. Offer small sips of room-temperature water (not ice-cold).
  3. Use a wet towel to cool paws, belly, and ears.
  4. Watch for signs of heatstroke: excessive panting, drooling, vomiting.
  5. In a fire, do not risk your life—call firefighters.
💧 Water Rescue (Drowning Dog) ▶

How to save a drowning dog:

  1. Reach with a pole, leash, or floatation device first.
  2. If entering water, approach from the side to avoid panic.
  3. Support the dog’s body—never lift by the collar.
  4. Check for water in lungs—hold small dogs upside down briefly.
  5. Wrap in a dry towel and keep warm.
🚗 Stray or Hit-by-Car Dog ▶

Helping a dog on the road:

  1. Park safely with hazard lights on.
  2. Use caution—injured dogs may bite.
  3. If the dog can move, guide them to safety with food or gentle calls.
  4. For severely injured dogs, call animal control or a vet.
  5. Check for a microchip at the nearest shelter.
