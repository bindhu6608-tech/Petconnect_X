Animal First Aid Guide

Immediate emergency care instructions for common injuries

Always contact a veterinarian after administering first aid!
CPR Cycle Timer: 00:00

Immediate First Aid Steps:

  1. Apply direct pressure with clean cloth or gauze
  2. Elevate the injured area if possible
  3. Maintain pressure for at least 5 minutes
  4. Apply cold compress around wound (not directly on)
Do NOT use tourniquet unless severe arterial bleeding

Emergency Treatment:

  1. Cool with room-temperature water for 10-15 minutes
  2. Cover with clean, non-stick dressing
  3. Do NOT apply ice or ointments
  4. Prevent animal from licking the burn
Chemical Burns: Flush with water for 20 minutes

Immediate Care:

  1. Restrict movement immediately
  2. Create makeshift splint with cardboard or magazine
  3. Apply cold pack to reduce swelling
  4. Transport carefully using rigid surface
Never try to realign the bone yourself

Emergency Response:

  1. Check mouth for visible obstruction
  2. Perform modified Heimlich maneuver:
    • Small animals: Hold upside down
    • Large animals: Abdominal thrusts
  3. Begin CPR if breathing stops
Risk of bite injuries - use caution

Immediate Actions:

  1. Identify toxin if possible
  2. Do NOT induce vomiting unless instructed
  3. Administer activated charcoal (if available)
  4. Collect vomit or stool sample for analysis
Contact Animal Poison Control immediately: 1-888-426-4435

Emergency Cooling:

  1. Move to cool area immediately
  2. Apply room-temperature water to body
  3. Use fan to enhance evaporation
  4. Offer small amounts of water to drink
Do NOT use ice water - can cause shock
