🐹 Hamster Rescue Guide

🚑 Injured Hamster ▶

⚠ Hamsters hide pain well - handle gently as they're fragile!

First aid for injured hamsters:

  1. Scoop up gently in cupped hands (never grab from above)
  2. Check for bleeding, limping, or labored breathing
  3. For wounds: clean with saline solution (no alcohol/hydrogen peroxide)
  4. Keep warm (78-80°F) - cold stress can be fatal
  5. Transport in small carrier with familiar bedding
  6. See exotic vet within 24 hours for any injury

🏠 Escaped Hamster ▶

Finding a lost hamster:

  1. Close all doors and block gaps under furniture
  2. Place cage on floor with food/water inside
  3. Set up "bucket trap" (stairs of books leading to deep bucket with bedding)
  4. Listen for scratching at night when most active
  5. Check warm places (behind appliances, in closets)
🍽 Malnourished/Dehydrated ▶

Helping underfed hamsters:

  1. Offer water via syringe (drip on lips if not drinking)
  2. Provide plain oatmeal or baby food (no onion/garlic)
  3. Introduce new foods slowly to avoid digestive upset
  4. Check teeth - overgrown teeth prevent eating
  5. Weigh daily to monitor progress
🤒 Sick Hamster Signs ▶

Emergency symptoms:

  1. Wet tail (diarrhea-stained rear) - fatal without treatment
  2. Lethargy lasting more than 24 hours
  3. Loud/wheezing breathing (respiratory infection)
  4. Lumps (abscesses or tumors common in older hamsters)
  5. Sudden weight loss or hunched posture
🐹 Abandoned Baby Hamsters ▶

Caring for orphans:

  1. Do NOT handle pinkies - mother may reject if human-scented
  2. Keep nest warm (95°F for newborns, reduce 5°F weekly)
  3. Feed puppy milk replacer via paintbrush every 2 hours
  4. Stimulate to urinate/defecate with warm damp cotton ball
  5. Introduce solid food at 2 weeks (ground pellets)
