🐴 Horse Rescue Guide

🚑 Injured Horse ▶

⚠ Approach injured horses with extreme caution - they may panic when in pain!

First aid for injured horses:

  1. Assess the situation safely before approaching
  2. Check for bleeding, lameness, or labored breathing
  3. For wounds: clean gently with saline solution
  4. Apply pressure to stop bleeding (use clean cloth)
  5. Keep the horse calm and still if possible
  6. Contact an equine veterinarian immediately

🏞 Trapped Horse ▶

Rescuing a trapped horse:

  1. Assess the situation for immediate dangers
  2. Keep the horse calm by speaking softly
  3. If in water/mud, place boards or branches for footing
  4. For fence entanglements, use bolt cutters carefully
  5. Have a halter and lead rope ready for control
  6. Call for professional help if needed
🍎 Malnourished Horse ▶

Rehabilitating underweight horses:

  1. Provide clean, fresh water at all times
  2. Start with high-quality hay (small frequent meals)
  3. Introduce concentrated feeds gradually
  4. Supplement with electrolytes if needed
  5. Monitor body condition weekly
  6. Check teeth for problems preventing eating
🤒 Sick Horse Signs ▶

Emergency symptoms:

  1. Colic signs (pawing, rolling, lack of manure)
  2. Labored breathing or nasal discharge
  3. Inability to stand
  4. Severe diarrhea or no feces for 24+ hours
  5. High fever (over 101.5°F)
  6. Neurological symptoms (staggering, head pressing)
🐴 Abandoned Foal ▶

Caring for orphaned foals:

  1. Keep warm (critical for newborns)
  2. Feed mare's milk replacer every 1-2 hours
  3. Ensure proper nursing position
  4. Stimulate to urinate/defecate if very young
  5. Introduce creep feed at 2-3 weeks old
  6. Provide companionship (another gentle horse)
